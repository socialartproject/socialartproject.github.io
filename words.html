The Compassionate Writer

The Compassionate Writer does not convince

The Compassionate Writer is subjective and personal

The Compassionate Writer is a listener

The Compassionate Writer writes for the person, not the idea

The Compassionate Writer is an exchange between just two people

The Compassionate Writer finds their Headspace before beginning

The Compassionate Writer is unafraid of judgement

The Compassionate Writer writes in the presence of others

The Compassionate Writer holds a clear conscience before and after Writing

The Compassionate Writer builds a life unhindered by ego

The Compassionate Writer only lifts words from one's own speech

The Compassionate Writer must only ever be responding

The Compassionate Writer declares bias without hesitation

The Compassionate Writer writes without regard for time

The Compassionate Writer is more patient than the most misfortunate crossing guard

The Compassionate Writer owns nothing

The Compassionate Writer is a search for Truth in its most mundane incarnation

The Compassionate Writer holds no repertoire

The Compassionate Writer, like schoolchildren, gives no thought to what is not

immediately present and vacuous and unsaid and

Cobwebs

As I clean out the last box from my room, I see a spider crawling through the threshold,
moving in.
He's renting the house from my family as they move across country and I move
across mine.

My family and I are going our separate ways,
citing creative differences.
I am John Lennon and they are the Beatles;
I am the most successful for now,
but I'll be dead soon and Paul will take my place.

The new tenant brings his family in,
swarms of a big, black pulsing crawling through cracks in the hardwood, through the windows, up from the sewer, into
the kitchen, the bedroom, the photo albums, the sock drawers,
the time I chased my sister into the banister, the broken dishwasher, all wriggling legs and little eyes, into the last time we eat dinner together, past the threshold, the empty space between my mother's heart and my father's grace.
They weave
a silken thread into the very fabric of life.

A vision, as I drive down the five with my car packed, away from home, towards home
of my car splintering open from the side, skidding across traffic-
my mangled corpse, bloodied with road rash, scattered amongst the landmarks of a life once lived.
Y'know, papers and such.

I am the man tasked with building the biggest enclosure in the world,
who constructs a small fence around himself and claims he's on the outside.
I guess it's all a matter of perspective.

I am exploring myself on the street, rummaging through the debris, looting the body.
I am suffering as the spider eats the beetle with uncanny precision.
I am building a fence and I am standing on the outside,
watching spiders spin webs, catching everything I love,
thinking I'm going to miss everything-
No, I'm going to miss everything
